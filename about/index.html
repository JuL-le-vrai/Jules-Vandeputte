About me

I'm a computer scientist at INRAE, in Montpellier, France.

I work as a research engineer at the Centre de Biologie et de Génétique de l'Environnement (CBGP).

My current research focuses on the application of machine learning, and more specifically deep learning techniques as a tool to developp new methodological approaches in population genetics, taking advantage of the large volume of data made available by the rise of next generation sequencing.

I'm particularly interested in the intersection of computer science and biology, and how machine learning can be used to address complex questions in population genetics.

I mainly work with new generation simulation based inference approaches, leveraging the power of deep learning to improve our understanding of genetic diversity and evolutionary processes. I'm also interested in designing neural network architectures adapted to the specificity of genomics data.

I'm a former member of the Pl@ntNet team, where I developed models of computer vision for the identification of plant diseases.
